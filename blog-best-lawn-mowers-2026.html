How to Choose

Yard Size

  • Under 1/4 acre: Electric push mower or robot mower. No need for gas
  • 1/4 to 1/2 acre: Self-propelled electric (EGO/Greenworks) or mid-range gas mower
  • 1/2 to 1 acre: Gas self-propelled or premium robot mower
  • Over 1 acre: Riding mower or zero-turn recommended

Electric vs Gas in 2026

Battery technology has caught up with gas in every category except runtime for large properties. Electric mowers start instantly, require virtually no maintenance (no oil changes, spark plugs, or fuel stabilizer), and produce zero emissions. Gas still wins for properties over 1/2 acre where a single battery charge isn't enough. The EGO Power+ has effectively closed the performance gap.

Maintenance Tips

  • Sharpen blades every 25 hours of mowing — dull blades tear grass, causing brown tips and disease susceptibility
  • Clean the deck after every mow — built-up grass clippings cause corrosion and reduce airflow
  • Never cut more than 1/3 of grass height at once — this stresses the plant and weakens the root system
  • Mow when dry — wet grass clumps, cuts unevenly, and clogs the deck
  • Alternate mowing patterns — changing direction each time prevents soil compaction and grass leaning

Frequently Asked Questions

Are battery lawn mowers as powerful as gas?

In 2026, premium battery mowers like the EGO Power+ match gas mowers in cutting power for yards under 1/2 acre. They start instantly, run quieter, and require less maintenance. Gas still has an edge for very large or hilly properties.

How long do robot mower batteries last?

Most robot mower batteries last 3-5 years before needing replacement. The mower itself can last 10+ years with proper maintenance. Replacement batteries typically cost $150-$300.

What is the best mowing height?

Most cool-season grasses (fescue, bluegrass) should be mowed at 3-3.5 inches. Warm-season grasses (Bermuda, zoysia) do best at 1-2 inches. Taller grass develops deeper roots and better drought resistance.

Is a self-propelled mower worth it?

If your yard has any slope or is larger than 1/4 acre, absolutely. Self-propelled mowers reduce fatigue significantly. Variable-speed models let you match the mower speed to your comfortable walking pace.

How often should I mow my lawn?

During peak growing season (spring/early summer), mow every 5-7 days. In summer heat, growth slows — mow every 7-10 days. Never remove more than 1/3 of the blade height in a single mow.
